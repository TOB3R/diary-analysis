Sizzle Reel

  • Episodes begin with ~ 1:30s - 2:00m sizzle reel consisting of engaging highlights of the episode. These clips are not related to the order in which they are discussed in the episode, it seems. Whatever is most engaging comes first.

  • There are several different sections of these introductions; different "in and out" points. The first 5 seconds of each episode is the first in and out. This is the first "gate" so-to-speak in the episode. We can imagine these "gates" as part of the sales pitch from the creator to the consumer. A "gate" is a moment in the episode where a viewer may tune out; it is the job of the edit to bring the viewer through each gate using creative & engaging techniques.

  • Each hook, each in and out use different techniques to bring viewers past each gate. They are seemingly editing the first 2 minutes of each episode in the style of short form content (TikTok, YT shorts). They get the viewer past the first gate with a 5 second edit, then a 10-20 second edit to pass the second gate, etc.. They ramp up the content length and context from short form to long from in the 2 minute intro until they get to the final long format of the show.


    - The initial hook can last anywhere from 5 seconds to 30 seconds
    - Next, the guest is introduced, quick bio
    - Third hook is introduced, leading question indicating discussion to be had
    - Fourth hook, guest reveals their platform and basis for their attendance
    - Fifth hook, rapid fire quick cuts of host and guest back and forth answering engaging questions, not expound upon
    - Appeal to emotion
    - CTA
    - Long-form

Subtitles

  • Subtitles are creatively placed throughout the reel to emphasize talking points and 'hooks'

  • Creative use of "Godfather" branding when talking to 'Godfather of AI'

    • Engages video audience
    • Important part of the initial 5 second hook

    • Host is represented by yellow text, while guest is represented by white
    • Always moving, fast paced cuts, zooms, blurs, effects
    • Subtitles are laid out in chunks or word by word, never the whole sentence at once
    • Placed all around the frame, much like a comic book rather than a novel

  • Kareoke-esque subtitles to punctuate engaging points

Edits

  • Sharp, quick, effective
  • Always moving. Often 5+ cuts in the first 10 seconds
  • Cut to different angle, b-roll, effect zoom, blur, wipe, transitions
    • Effect usage

  • Often opens on split-screen of guest and host, pseudo thumbnail

  • Moving background vfx to entertain video audience

  • Creative B-roll to illustrate the guest's monologue and keep up pace of visual stimulus

Titles

Theme

  • The mysterious but playful instrumental theme is usually played at the beginning of the longform segment of the show, marking the transition. Even if viewers skip the 2 minute introduction using the timestamps, they will always hear the theme at the start of the longform section. This creates another component that the show can be identified by.
